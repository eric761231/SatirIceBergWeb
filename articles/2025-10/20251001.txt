Wednesday                                                Week Twenty Three

在人生的每一刻裡，你都是站在一個十字路口上，生命挑戰你去選擇一條路徑……
在日本武術中，這種時刻的挑戰是以兩把劍的傳統來代表的。一把是「賦與生命的劍」，
叫做「活人劍」（katsujinken）；另一把是「奪取生命的劍」，叫做「殺人刀」（satsujinoto）。
在你的人生道路上，每時每刻，你都會抽出其中一把劍。當你做出一個肯定生命的選擇
或採取一個肯定生命的行動時，你抽出的是「賦與生命的劍」。當你出於自我中心、
無意識的習慣、恐懼或反應而行動時，你抽出的是「奪取生命的劍」。你流血而不是
滋養自己，耗盡了你真正精神的活力。

一位偉大的心理學家 Abraham Maslow 馬斯洛曾經說過：「想像人生是一系列選擇的過程，
一個緊接一個。在每一個時點，都有一個往前進的選擇和一個向後退的選擇。若一天當中
有十數次的選擇是有關成長的選擇，而非有關恐懼的選擇，那麼那一天就有十數次向自我實現。」

——Joel Levy

Moment to moment you stand at a crossroads and life challenges you to choose a path....
In Japanese martial arts, this moment-to-moment challenge is represented in the tradition 
of the two swords. One is "the sword that gives life," katsujinken; the other is "the sword 
that takes life," satsujinoto. Moment-to-moment on your path of life, you draw one of these 
swords. When you make a life-affirming choice of take a life-affirming action, you draw 
"the sword that gives life." When you act out of self-centeredness, mindless habit, fear, 
or reactivity, you draw "the sword that takes life." You bleed instead of feed yourself, 
depleting the vitality of your true spirit.

The great psychologist Abraham Maslow once said, "Think of life as a process of choices, 
one after another. At each point, there is a progression choice and a regression choice. 
To make the growth choice instead of the fear choice a dozen times a day is to move a dozen 
times towards self-actualization."

——Joel Levy
